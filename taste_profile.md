# TASTE PROFILE — "오늘의 발견" Newsletter Curation Brief

This file is the single source of truth for what to surface in the daily newsletter.
The curator (AI) reads this and finds things the reader does NOT already know,
but that fit this sensibility. Update freely — just edit the lists below.

---

## CORE IDENTITY (the through-line)
The reader sits at the intersection of:
- **Ura-Harajuku lineage** (裏原宿) — NIGO, Fujiwara Hiroshi, Jun Takahashi, the 90s Tokyo street machine
- **End-of-century Japan** (世紀末日本) — the specific melancholy + energy of 90s/early-00s Tokyo
- **90s New York & LA** — hip-hop, skate, graffiti, the grimy-glamorous duality
- **Old car culture** — analog machines, boxy 80s/90s steel, patina over polish
- **Underground / subculture** — the minor, the overlooked, the insider-only

The unifying thread: **authenticity, patina, lineage, and knowing the thing behind the thing.**
Not what's trending — what's *referenced by* what's trending.

## CURATION RULES (how to pick each day)
1. **Novelty filter:** assume the reader ALREADY knows the famous names above.
   Surface the adjacent-but-unknown: the lesser-known piece, the side player,
   the precursor, the deep cut. The names above are the *coordinate system*, not the answers.
2. **One genuine discovery per category** beats six obvious links.
   Some days a category can be skipped if nothing good — quality over completeness.
3. **Tone of the "why":** explain the lineage. Connect it back to something the reader loves.
   "If you like X, this is the thing X came from."
4. **No hype-cycle filler.** Avoid generic "this week's drops" unless genuinely fitting the taste.
5. **Respect the AVOID list.** No womenswear, no formal tailoring, no quiet-luxury-without-roots.
6. **Bias toward:** patina, provenance, the underground, Japan, the analog, the referential.
